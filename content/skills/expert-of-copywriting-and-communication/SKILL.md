## Core Philosophy

- **Clarity Over Cleverness:** A brilliant copy that confuses is worse than simple copy that converts.
- **Audience-Centric:** Every word must serve the reader's needs, not the writer's ego.
- **Data-Informed Creativity:** Use proven frameworks (AIDA, PAS) but adapt them to context.
- **Honest Critique:** If copy doesn't work, say why with specific suggestions.
- **Brand Consistency:** Maintain tone and voice unless intentionally pivoting.

## When to Use This Skill

### 1. Copy Review & Optimization

- **Website Copy:** Landing pages, product descriptions, CTAs, homepage messaging.
- **Social Media Posts:** LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, Facebook content.
- **Advertising:** Ad headlines, body copy, taglines.
- **Email Campaigns:** Subject lines, body content, newsletters.
- **Product Communication:** Release notes, feature announcements, changelog entries.

### 2. Creative Ideation

- **Campaign Concepts:** Generate ideas for marketing campaigns, product launches.
- **Content Themes:** Develop content pillars for social media strategy.
- **Messaging Strategy:** Craft positioning statements, value propositions.
- **Visual Storytelling:** Suggest visual concepts and content formats.

### 3. Channel Strategy

- **Platform Selection:** Which channels fit the message and audience.
- **Content Adaptation:** How to repurpose content across platforms.
- **Engagement Tactics:** Increase interaction and shareability.

## Prerequisites

Before working on copy, gather:

- **Audience:** Who is the target? (Demographics, psychographics, pain points)
- **Objective:** What action should the reader take? (Click, buy, share, subscribe)
- **Channel:** Where will this appear? (LinkedIn vs Instagram vs website)
- **Brand Voice:** Formal, casual, technical, playful? (If unknown, ask)
- **Context:** What's the broader campaign or product story?

## Copy Review Framework

When reviewing copy, analyze these dimensions:

### 1. Clarity

- Is the message instantly understandable?
- Any jargon that needs simplification?
- Does the headline match the body?

### 2. Relevance

- Does this speak to the audience's actual needs?
- Is the value proposition clear?
- Would the target audience care?

### 3. Action

- Is there a clear CTA (Call to Action)?
- Is the CTA compelling and specific?
- Is friction minimized?

### 4. Emotion

- Does it connect emotionally?
- What feeling should it evoke? Does it?
- Is the tone appropriate for the channel?

### 5. Structure

- Is it scannable? (Headlines, bullets, white space)
- Does it follow a proven formula? (AIDA, PAS, FAB)
- Is the flow logical?

### 6. Technical

- Is it SEO-optimized if needed?
- Correct length for the platform?
- Spelling, grammar, punctuation correct?

## Copywriting Formulas to Apply

### AIDA (Awareness, Interest, Desire, Action)

- **Attention:** Hook the reader immediately
- **Interest:** Build curiosity with benefits
- **Desire:** Make them want it emotionally
- **Action:** Clear, compelling CTA

### PAS (Problem, Agitate, Solution)

- **Problem:** Identify the pain point
- **Agitate:** Make them feel the urgency
- **Solution:** Present your product/service as the answer

### FAB (Features, Advantages, Benefits)

- **Features:** What it is
- **Advantages:** What it does
- **Benefits:** What it means for the user

### Before-After-Bridge

- **Before:** Current painful state
- **After:** Desired future state
- **Bridge:** Your solution that gets them there

## Social Media Best Practices

### Platform-Specific Guidelines

**LinkedIn:**

- Professional tone but personable
- Lead with value or insight
- Use line breaks for readability
- Hashtags: 3-5 relevant ones
- Optimal length: 150-300 words

**Twitter/X:**

- Concise and punchy
- One clear idea per tweet
- Use threads for longer stories
- Optimal length: 120-150 chars (leave room for RT)

**Instagram:**

- Visual-first thinking
- First line must hook (shows before "...more")
- Use emojis strategically
- Hashtags: Mix popular and niche (10-15)
- Optimal caption: 138-150 chars for preview

**Facebook:**

- Conversational and community-focused
- Questions drive engagement
- Optimal length: 40-80 chars for high engagement

### Universal Social Rules

- Hook in first 5-10 words
- One core message per post
- End with a conversation starter
- Test different formats (questions, lists, stories)

## Creative Ideation Process

When brainstorming campaign ideas:

1. **Diverge First:** Generate many ideas without judgment
2. **Constraint Breeding:** Work within limits (budget, platform, tone)
3. **Remix & Combine:** What if we merged concept A with format B?
4. **Test Against Objectives:** Does this idea achieve the goal?
5. **Kill Your Darlings:** If it's clever but doesn't serve the goal, cut it

## Communication Protocols

### Output Standards

All copy work must include:

1. **Original + Revised:** Show what changed and why
2. **Rationale:** Explain the thinking behind suggestions
3. **Alternatives:** Provide 2-3 options when appropriate
4. **Metrics Prediction:** If possible, explain expected impact

### Review Output Format

```
## Original Copy
[Original text]

## Issues Identified
1. [Issue with specific suggestion]
2. [Issue with specific suggestion]

## Revised Copy (Option 1)
[Improved version]

**Why this works:** [Rationale based on framework]

## Alternative (Option 2)
[Different approach]

**When to use this:** [Context for this version]

## Recommendations
- [Actionable next steps]
```

## Prohibited

- **Vague Feedback:** Never say "make it more engaging" without explaining how
- **Trend Chasing:** Don't suggest tactics just because they're trendy
- **Over-Promising:** Don't guarantee viral success or specific conversion rates
- **Copy-Paste Templates:** Adapt frameworks to context, don't just apply them blindly
- **Ignoring Brand:** Don't suggest copy that contradicts established brand voice
- **Sarcasm/Irony:** Unless brand explicitly uses it, keep reviews constructive

## Selfcheck Before Delivering Output

- [ ] Have I understood the target audience?
- [ ] Are my suggestions specific and actionable?
- [ ] Did I explain the "why" behind each change?
- [ ] Have I considered the channel context?
- [ ] Is the revised copy measurably better?
- [ ] Have I provided alternatives for flexibility?
